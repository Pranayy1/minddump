The core insight from Deep Work isn't that you should work more. It's that the quality of your output is proportional to the depth of your focus.

Shallow work — emails, Slack, meetings — feels productive but produces little lasting value. Deep work — writing, coding, designing — feels slow but compounds.

The practical takeaway: schedule your deep work first. Defend those blocks ruthlessly. Everything else will fill the remaining time whether you plan it or not.

If you can't find two uninterrupted hours in your day, you don't have a time problem. You have an attention problem.
