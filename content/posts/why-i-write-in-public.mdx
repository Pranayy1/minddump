---
title: "Why I Write in Public"
date: "2026-06-20"
summary: "On the uncomfortable practice of publishing before you feel ready, and why the act of sharing accelerates learning more than private study ever could."
---

Writing in public is terrifying at first. Every post feels like admitting you don't know something. But that's exactly the point.

The gap between what you think you understand and what you can articulate is where real learning happens. When you write for an audience — even an imaginary one — you can't hand-wave past the parts you don't fully get. You have to make the thinking legible.

I started this site because I was tired of accumulating bookmarks and half-finished notes that never went anywhere. The commitment to publish, even imperfectly, turns passive consumption into active synthesis.

Some things I've learned so far:

- You don't need to be an expert to write about a topic. You need to be honest about where you are.
- The best posts come from genuine confusion, not from having all the answers.
- Publishing regularly matters more than publishing perfectly.

The name of this site — Minddump — is a reminder. Dump your mind. Build your knowledge.
